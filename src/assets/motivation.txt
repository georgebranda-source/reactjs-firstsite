Canadians have long grappled with questions of identity as a result of their unique position in the world economy. Determined to resist the economic pull of the United States, nation-building politicians such as Pierre Trudeau and John Diefenbaker have long fought to diversify its trading partners. Despite this recurring political theme, results were mixed, with Canada's economic reliance on the United States only growing throughout the neoliberal era.

In 2025, Donald Trump's tariff threats and '51st State' assertions showed that dependence on the American market has silently corroded Canada's independence. As a result, multipartisan efforts to strengthen Canadian sovereignty by diversifying its trading partners have begun with renewed intensity. For example, Mark Carney's January 2026 visit to China, which led the two countries to declare tariff reductions on a wide range of goods. Considering the importance of Canada's trade relationships to its national independence, this site intends to document the effects of these changes and more in as close to real-time as possible.
